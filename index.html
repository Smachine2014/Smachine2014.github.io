ABOUT
NORTH SHORE JUDO

The North Shore Judo Academy was founded in 1969 by Sensei Ron Jones (5th dan) and has shared a love of Judo with thousands of players in that time, including the current coaching team.

Now known as North Shore Judo, Simon Greenhill became coach in 2018. Simon started his Judo aged 5, then became a Judo New Zealand recognized National Club Coach in 2006 along with his Sho Dan. North Shore Judo club members are actively involved in local and international competitions.

Can I come and watch a class?
Of course! We encourage anyone interested in judo, whether potential members or curious observers, to come and watch a class. This gives you a firsthand look at our training environment and teaching methods. Feel free to visit, ask questions, and experience the dynamic atmosphere at North Shore Judo.
Am I too old to start Judo?
Absolutely not! Judo is a sport for people of all ages. Whether you're a teenager or a senior, you can start learning and enjoying the benefits of judo. Our classes are designed to accommodate individuals at different stages of life, ensuring everyone can participate and progress at their own pace.
Will I get hurt?
Our skilled coaches prioritize safety through guided training and proper technique, ensuring you can enjoy judo with minimized risks. Your safety is our topmost priority at North Shore Judo.
